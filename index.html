Adolf Hitler

Leader of the Nazi Party and Dictator of Germany (1933–1945)

A historical portrait of Adolf Hitler during his tenure as Chancellor.

Brief Historical Timeline

  • 1889: Born in Braunau am Inn, Austria-Hungary.
  • 1908: Moved to Vienna in an unsuccessful attempt to pursue a career as a fine artist.
  • 1914: Enlisted in the Bavarian Army at the outbreak of World War I, serving as a dispatch runner on the Western Front.
  • 1919: Joined the German Workers' Party (DAP), which later became the NSDAP (Nazi Party).
  • 1921: Became the official chairman and undisputed leader of the Nazi Party.
  • 1923: Attempted a coup known as the Beer Hall Putsch; arrested and imprisoned, during which he wrote Mein Kampf.
  • 1933: Appointed Chancellor of Germany, quickly consolidating power into a totalitarian dictatorship following the Reichstag fire.
  • 1934: Merged the offices of President and Chancellor following the death of Paul von Hindenburg, declaring himself Führer.
  • 1935: Instituted the Nuremberg Laws, systematically stripping German Jews of their citizenship and civil rights.
  • 1938: Orchestrated the annexation of Austria (Anschluss) and parts of Czechoslovakia under the policy of territorial expansion.
  • 1939: Ordered the invasion of Poland, initiating World War II in Europe and beginning the implementation of the Holocaust.
  • 1941: Launched Operation Barbarossa, the massive invasion of the Soviet Union, and declared war on the United States.
  • 1944: Survived an assassination attempt led by German military officers in the July 20 plot.
  • 1945: Committed suicide in his Berlin bunker as Soviet forces advanced on the city, leading to Germany's unconditional surrender.

Associated Historical Figures & Family

Eva Braun, (Wife of Adolf Hitler)

  • 1912: Born in Munich, Germany.
  • 1929: Met Adolf Hitler in Munich while working as an assistant and model for his personal photographer, Heinrich Hoffmann.
  • 1932: Became Hitler's primary companion, surviving a suicide attempt during their early relationship.
  • 1936: Moved into Hitler's household at the Berghof near Berchtesgaden, living as a heavily shielded and private figure throughout WWII.
  • 1945: Traveled to Berlin against orders to join Hitler in the Führerbunker; married him on April 29, and committed suicide alongside him via cyanide on April 30.

Alois Hitler Sr., (Father of Adolf Hitler)

  • 1837: Born out of wedlock to Maria Schicklgruber in Strones, Austria.
  • 1855: Entered the Austrian customs service, climbing the ranks to become a minor customs official.
  • 1876: Legally changed his surname from Schicklgruber to Hitler after his stepfather, Johann Georg Hiedler, recognized him.
  • 1885: Married his third wife, Klara Pölzl, following a special papal dispensation due to their close familial relationship.
  • 1903: Died suddenly from a pleural hemorrhage in Leonding, Austria, at the age of 65.

Klara Hitler, (Mother of Adolf Hitler)

  • 1860: Born Klara Pölzl in Spital, Austria-Hungary.
  • 1885: Married Alois Hitler Sr., subsequently giving birth to six children, though only Adolf and Paula survived to adulthood.
  • 1903: Widowed by Alois, remaining dedicated to raising her children under difficult economic circumstances.
  • 1907: Diagnosed with breast cancer; passed away under the medical care of Dr. Eduard Bloch in Linz, Austria.

Alois Hitler Jr. (Elder half-brother of Adolf Hitler)

  • 1882: Born Alois Matzelsberger in Vienna, the illegitimate son of Alois Hitler Sr. and Franziska Matzelsberger (later legitimized).
  • 1910: Moved to Dublin, Ireland, marrying Bridget Dowling, with whom he had a son, William Patrick Hitler.
  • 1914: Left his family to return to Germany; established a second, bigamous marriage and had a son, Heinz Hitler, who later died in Soviet captivity.
  • 1934: Opened a popular restaurant in Berlin that became a frequent hangout for Nazi officials during the regime.
  • 1956: Died in Hamburg, Germany, outliving his younger half-brother by eleven years.

Angela Hitler, (Elder half-sister of Adolf Hitler)

  • 1883: Born in Vienna to Alois Hitler Sr. and his second wife, Franziska Matzelsberger.
  • 1903: Married Leo Raubal Sr., with whom she had three children, including her daughter Geli Raubal.
  • 1928: Moved to the Berghof to act as housekeeper for her half-brother Adolf; left in 1935 following strained relations.
  • 1936: Married German architect Martin Hammitzsch, distancing herself further from Berlin's inner political circle.
  • 1949: Died of a stroke in Hanover, West Germany, following the end of the post-war reconstruction period.
